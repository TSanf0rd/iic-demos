Resolve the BOM

A configured case's raw bill of materials isn't the parts on the case — it's a ledger of additions and deletions. Every kit upgrade leaves the standard part behind as a −1 delete line and adds its replacement as a +1. Resolving the BOM means netting those out to the parts that were actually shipped, then narrowing to the parts a refresh touches. This is the airtight answer to "how do you know these parts fit my case?"

What just happened, at real scale. George's numbers: a real configured case returns roughly 1,000 raw BOM lines, nets to about 800 shipped parts, and filters to roughly 20 refresh-relevant parts. Nobody resolves this today — it's the standout, demonstrable data-engineering step. The whole transformation is one grouped sum (net qty per part) plus two filters (drop qty == 0, then keep refresh categories). Those — refresh parts are exactly what feeds the AI kit-matcher next.
