Step 3 · AI Parts Matching

Group parts into kits

The resolved BOM gives us the net parts on the case. But a rep shouldn't quote seven loose SKUs — they should quote kits. A kit is the unit of refresh: a bumper kit bundles the bumper, bracket, hanger, and fasteners. This step is the inverse of netting — it groups parts up into kits. One part can belong to more than one kit, and when it does, the math reconciles with the resolved quantity.

Input · resolved refresh parts (from bom_resolver)
Assign each resolved part to the kit(s) it belongs to, then roll up per kit.
Why this matters for the pitch. Seven parts became 2 kits — that's the "think in kits, not parts" story, and it's what a customer or rep actually selects. Each kit still explodes to its parts (expand a card to see them), so nothing is hidden — the quoter can verify every SKU. The parts subtotal here is parts only; the full quote (size scaling, case labor ≈ $150/linear ft, national-tier discount) is computed by the quote engine in Step 4 — kept separate on purpose so lifecycle, parts, and pricing never bleed into each other.
